# Under-three-minute demo

## Before the clock

- `./scripts/doctor.sh` passes.
- Zoom is signed in and set to Robin’s two virtual devices.
- Control panel is open from the operator’s private machine.
- A second participant has the meeting open and can admit Robin.
- The dedicated workspace contains only a blank TextEdit window.

## Script

**0:00–0:25** — Paste the meeting link and press Join. Robin launches Zoom, connects computer audio, and waits for admission.

**0:25–0:55** — Say: “Robin, introduce yourself in one sentence.” Interrupt halfway with: “Actually, keep it to five words.” Show that queued speech stops promptly.

**0:55–1:45** — Say: “Create a local TextEdit note titled Demo Result with three launch checklist bullets, verify it, then share the workspace.” Robin delegates to GPT-5.6, completes the note, and starts normal Zoom sharing.

**1:45–2:15** — Ask Robin to explain the result. Show the intended workspace in Zoom and the live redacted action trace in the private panel.

**2:15–2:40** — Ask Robin to stop sharing and leave. Confirm Zoom returns to Ready.

**2:40–3:00** — Point to the health panel and trace. Mention that sending or publishing requires exact-action approval and blocked classes cannot be approved.

If any recovery budget is exhausted, demonstrate human takeover instead of improvising unsafe clicks.
